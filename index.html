Program Overview

ACE Summer 2026 is a summer-only program for middle school students in grades 6–8. This website is for high school interns to quickly understand what they are facilitating each day, what materials they need, and how to lead activities with confidence.

Morning Team

AM STEAM Specialists

Lead hands-on science, technology, engineering, art, and design challenges. Focus on curiosity, teamwork, problem-solving, and safe use of materials.

Afternoon Team

PM Enrichment Specialists

Lead movement, sports, cooking, team games, recreation, arts, and community-building activities. Focus on energy, fun, safety, and connection.

AM STEAM Specialist Portal

Monday: Team Engineering

Activity: Marshmallow Tower Challenge

Goal: Campers practice teamwork, planning, and building.

Materials: Marshmallows, spaghetti, tape, timer.

  1. Split campers into small teams.
  2. Explain the building challenge and safety expectations.
  3. Give teams 20–30 minutes to build.
  4. Measure towers and celebrate effort.
  5. Debrief what worked and what was hard.

Tuesday: Science Lab

Activity: Sink or Float Design Challenge

Goal: Campers test materials and revise their designs.

Materials: Foil, pennies, water bins, paper towels.

Wednesday: Tech & Design

Activity: Digital Poster or Logo Design

Goal: Campers create a visual identity for their team.

Materials: Tablets/computers, paper, markers.

Thursday: Build & Test

Activity: Catapult or Paper Bridge Challenge

Goal: Campers learn through testing, failure, and redesign.

Friday: Showcase Prep

Activity: Team Presentations

Goal: Campers explain what they built and what they learned.

PM Enrichment Specialist Portal

Monday: Movement & Team Games

Activity: Relay Races + Team Challenges

Goal: Campers build energy, teamwork, and communication.

Materials: Cones, balls, jump ropes, music.

Tuesday: Sports Rotation

Activity: Basketball, soccer, kickball, or field games.

Goal: Campers practice teamwork, sportsmanship, and movement.

Wednesday: Cooking Lab

Activity: No-bake snacks or simple cooking demo.

Goal: Campers learn food safety, teamwork, and basic life skills.

Materials: Gloves, wipes, ingredients, serving supplies.

Thursday: Creative Enrichment

Activity: Art, dance, music, photography, or media project.

Goal: Campers express themselves and build confidence.

Friday: Field Day / Camp Challenge

Activity: Full-group games, competitions, and reflection.

Goal: End the week with joy, teamwork, and celebration.

Sample Daily Flow

Morning Block: STEAM

  • Welcome + instructions
  • Hands-on challenge
  • Build/test/revise
  • Clean up materials
  • Reflection question

Afternoon Block: Enrichment

  • Movement warm-up
  • Main enrichment activity
  • Team challenge or game
  • Water/rest break
  • Closing reflection

Intern Toolkit

How to Start an Activity

  • Get everyone’s attention before explaining.
  • Use simple directions.
  • Show an example before campers begin.
  • Ask: “What questions do you have?”

Behavior Support

  • Stay calm and clear.
  • Redirect privately when possible.
  • Give choices instead of arguing.
  • Call adult staff if safety becomes a concern.

Backup Activities

  • Four Corners
  • Would You Rather
  • Silent Ball
  • Rock Paper Scissors Tournament
  • Team Name + Team Chant
